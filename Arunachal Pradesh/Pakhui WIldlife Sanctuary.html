Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary

Discover the Rich Biodiversity of Arunachal Pradesh’s Hidden Gem

Overview

Nestled in the East Kameng District of Arunachal Pradesh, Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary, also known as Pakke Wildlife Sanctuary, is a pristine natural haven covering an area of 862 square kilometers. This sanctuary was established in 1977 and is recognized for its incredible biodiversity and ecological significance. It is characterized by its lush green landscapes, which include dense tropical rainforests, lush grasslands, and rolling hills. The sanctuary is bordered by the Kemeng River to the west and the Pakke River to the east, creating a rich mosaic of natural habitats that support a diverse range of flora and fauna. This protected area is not only a refuge for numerous wildlife species but also an important site for scientific research and conservation efforts aimed at preserving the unique biodiversity of the Eastern Himalayas.

Flora and Fauna

Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary is renowned for its rich biodiversity, offering a home to a wide array of plant and animal species. The sanctuary’s flora includes dense forests of sal and teak trees, interspersed with giant bamboos, lush undergrowth, and vibrant orchids. The diverse vegetation supports a range of wildlife, including some of the most elusive and endangered species. Among the mammalian inhabitants are the Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris), the Clouded Leopard (Neofelis nebulosa), and the Indian Wild Dog (Cuon alpinus). Visitors may also encounter the Indian Elephant (Elephas maximus), which roams the sanctuary’s forests in search of food and water. The sanctuary is a birdwatcher’s paradise, featuring species such as the Great Hornbill (Buceros bicornis) and the White-winged Wood Duck (Asarcornis scutulata). Additionally, the sanctuary is home to various reptiles, including the Python (Python molurus) and the Indian Cobra (Naja naja), adding to the rich tapestry of life within this protected area.

The sanctuary’s varied ecosystems also support a range of smaller mammals, such as the Jungle Cat (Felis chaus), the Rhesus Macaque (Macaca mulatta), and the Flying Squirrel (Petaurista spp.). Reptiles like the Krait (Bungarus spp.) and various species of frogs and lizards also inhabit the sanctuary’s diverse habitats. The sanctuary’s rich biodiversity makes it a vital area for conservation efforts and a significant location for ecological studies, helping to deepen our understanding of the natural world.

Location

Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the East Kameng District of Arunachal Pradesh, a state situated in the northeastern region of India. It is positioned approximately 280 kilometers from Itanagar, the state capital, and is easily accessible from various transportation hubs in the region. The sanctuary is nestled within the eastern Himalayan foothills, which contribute to its varied terrain that includes dense forests, rolling hills, and numerous rivers. The geographic coordinates of Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary are approximately 27.1°N latitude and 92.7°E longitude. This strategic location places the sanctuary within the broader Eastern Himalayas biodiversity hotspot, a region recognized for its high levels of endemism and rich natural heritage. The sanctuary’s location in this ecologically significant area helps to support a diverse range of plant and animal life, making it a key area for conservation and ecological research.

Best Time To Visit

The optimal time to visit Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary is during the cooler months from October to March. During this period, the weather is generally dry and pleasant, with daytime temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C. These conditions are ideal for exploring the sanctuary’s rich biodiversity and participating in wildlife observation activities. The post-monsoon months from October to December offer clear skies and good visibility, while the winter months from December to February provide cooler temperatures that encourage animals to be more active. The sanctuary is closed during the monsoon season, from April to September, due to heavy rains that can lead to challenging travel conditions and increased risk of landslides.

Vegetation

The vegetation in Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary is characterized by a rich and diverse array of plant species that create a vibrant and complex ecosystem. The sanctuary’s forests are dominated by tall trees such as the sal (Shorea robusta) and teak (Tectona grandis), which form a dense canopy that supports a variety of undergrowth plants. The sanctuary also features extensive stands of giant bamboo, which are a significant feature of the landscape. Other notable plant species include various orchids, ferns, and shrubs that thrive in the moist forest environment. The sanctuary’s vegetation supports a wide range of wildlife and plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of the region. The diverse plant life also contributes to the scenic beauty of the sanctuary, offering visitors a lush and picturesque natural setting to explore.

Travelling

Traveling to Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary is relatively straightforward, with several transportation options available for visitors. The nearest airport to the sanctuary is located in Tezpur, which is approximately 70 kilometers away. From Tezpur, visitors can take a taxi or local bus to reach the sanctuary. The nearest major railway station is Rangapara, situated about 55 kilometers from the sanctuary, with regular train services connecting it to major cities such as Guwahati and New Delhi. For those traveling by road, regular bus services are available from Tezpur to the sanctuary, offering a scenic drive through the beautiful landscapes of Arunachal Pradesh. Additionally, private taxis and jeeps can be hired from Tezpur or Rangapara for a more comfortable and flexible travel experience. It is advisable to check road conditions and make transportation arrangements in advance, especially during the peak tourist season.

Accommodation

Accommodation options near Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary cater to a range of preferences and budgets, ensuring a comfortable stay for all visitors. Within the sanctuary, the Forest Rest House at Pakke offers basic amenities and a rustic experience, allowing guests to stay close to nature. The rest house is a popular choice for those looking to immerse themselves in the sanctuary’s serene environment. In addition to the rest house, there are a few eco-lodges and guesthouses in the nearby towns of Seijosa and Itanagar. These accommodations provide a range of facilities, including comfortable rooms, dining options, and guided tours of the sanctuary. It is recommended to book accommodations well in advance, particularly during the peak season from October to March, to ensure availability and secure the best rates.

Photography and Wildlife Watching Tips

For enthusiasts interested in wildlife photography and observation, Pakhui Wildlife Sanctuary offers numerous opportunities to capture stunning images and observe a variety of animal species. Early morning and late afternoon are the best times for wildlife sightings, as animals are more active during these periods. A good quality camera with a telephoto lens will enhance your chances of photographing distant or elusive wildlife. It is also important to wear neutral-colored clothing and maintain a quiet demeanor to avoid disturbing the animals. Guided safari tours are available and can provide valuable insights into the sanctuary’s wildlife and habitats. Participating in these tours and following ethical wildlife observation practices will help you make the most of your visit and contribute to the preservation of this incredible natural environment.
