Aga Khan Palace

A symbol of India's freedom struggle

Historical Significance

Aga Khan Palace, built in 1892 by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III, served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi, his wife Kasturba Gandhi, his secretary Mahadev Desai, and Sarojini Naidu during the Indian freedom struggle. It holds immense historical importance as a witness to their sacrifices and contributions to India's independence movement.

Cultural Significance

Aga Khan Palace stands as a symbol of India's struggle for freedom and the resilience of its people against colonial rule. It has been declared as a monument of national importance by the Archaeological Survey of India. Today, it serves as a museum dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi and his life, attracting visitors from all over the world.

Brief Synopsis

Aga Khan Palace, located in Pune, Maharashtra, is a historical landmark that played a pivotal role in India's fight for independence. Constructed in 1892, the palace served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi and other freedom fighters during the British Raj. It now stands as a museum and memorial, preserving the legacy of India's struggle for freedom.

Integrity

The integrity of Aga Khan Palace is well-maintained, with efforts to preserve its architectural and historical significance. Conservation work ensures the physical fabric of the palace remains intact, while measures are in place to protect it from environmental degradation and other threats.

Authenticity

Aga Khan Palace retains its authenticity, reflecting the conditions of its historical significance. Restoration efforts have been undertaken to ensure that the palace maintains its original qualities and design, allowing visitors to experience the essence of India's freedom struggle.
