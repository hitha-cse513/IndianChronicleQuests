About Tso Moriri

Tso Moriri, located in the Changthang region of Ladakh, is a high-altitude lake nestled amidst majestic mountains and sprawling meadows. This pristine lake is known for its crystal-clear blue waters that reflect the surrounding snow-capped peaks, creating a picturesque landscape that mesmerizes visitors. The lake is an important breeding ground for various species of migratory birds, making it a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. Surrounding Tso Moriri are traditional Changpa nomadic settlements, where visitors can experience the unique culture and lifestyle of the region. The serene atmosphere and breathtaking views make Tso Moriri a must-visit destination for those seeking tranquility and natural beauty in Ladakh.

Local Attractions

Explore nearby attractions that enhance the experience of visiting Tso Moriri:

Korzok Monastery

Korzok Monastery, situated on the northern banks of Tso Moriri, is one of the highest-altitude monasteries in India. Founded in the 17th century, this monastery is known for its ancient murals, Buddhist scriptures, and annual Korzok Gustor Festival, where monks perform sacred masked dances. The monastery offers panoramic views of Tso Moriri and serves as a spiritual center for the local community, preserving centuries-old traditions amidst the stunning Himalayan landscape.

Changthang Wildlife Sanctuary

Changthang Wildlife Sanctuary, surrounding Tso Moriri, is a sanctuary for endangered wildlife species such as Tibetan wild ass (kiang), Tibetan gazelle, snow leopard, and various migratory birds. The sanctuary offers opportunities for wildlife spotting and trekking amidst vast grasslands and rugged terrain, providing a glimpse into Ladakh’s diverse ecosystem and natural beauty.

Namashang La Pass

Namashang La Pass, located near Tso Moriri, is a scenic mountain pass offering breathtaking views of the Changthang Plateau and its surrounding peaks. The pass is a popular trekking route for adventurers seeking challenging trails and stunning vistas of Ladakh’s remote wilderness.

Location

Tso Moriri is located in the Changthang region of Ladakh, approximately 250 kilometers southeast of Leh. It is accessible via a scenic drive through rugged mountain terrain and high-altitude passes, offering adventurous travelers a glimpse into Ladakh’s remote and untouched landscapes.

The climate around Tso Moriri is cold desert, characterized by harsh winters and mild summers. Visitors should prepare for fluctuating temperatures and carry adequate warm clothing, especially if traveling during the winter months.

Reaching Tso Moriri

Air

The nearest airport to Tso Moriri is Leh's Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport. From Leh, visitors can travel to Tso Moriri by road, which takes approximately 7-8 hours depending on road conditions and weather.

Road

Tso Moriri is accessible by road from Leh via Upshi and Mahe. The journey offers stunning views of Ladakh’s rugged landscapes and requires careful planning due to high-altitude conditions and limited facilities along the route.

Trekking

For adventurous travelers, trekking routes from Leh to Tso Moriri offer a unique experience to explore Ladakh’s remote wilderness on foot, passing through scenic valleys and high mountain passes.
