About Shillong

Nestled amidst the pine-clad hills, Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, unfolds like a picturesque canvas at an elevation of 1496 meters. Revered as the 'Scotland of the East,' the city offers a delightful blend of captivating landscapes, pleasant weather, and rich traditions. Shillong derives its name from Lei Shyllong, an idol worshipped at the Shillong Peak, and is home to diverse tribal communities like Khyrim, Mylliem, Maharam, Mallaisohmat, Bhowal, and Langrim, each contributing to the vibrant cultural tapestry.

Shillong boasts a variety of attractions that cater to nature enthusiasts and cultural explorers alike. Umiam Lake, surrounded by hills, offers a tranquil retreat, while the Elephant Falls cascades amidst lush greenery, adding to the city's natural allure. For adventure seekers, the Laitlum Canyons offer breathtaking panoramic views, perfect for trekking enthusiasts. The city also houses cultural gems like the Don Bosco Museum and the Meghalaya State Museum, showcasing the Northeast's indigenous cultures and heritage. The bustling Police Bazar captures Shillong's lively atmosphere, offering diverse shopping experiences. Beyond the city, Mawphlang's sacred groves and traditional rituals showcase Meghalaya's cultural heritage, while Mawlynnong, known as the cleanest village in Asia, beckons with its living root bridges and pristine landscapes. Shillong's musical heartbeat resonates through its title as the "music capital of India," with a dynamic music scene celebrated through various music events and festivals throughout the year. The warmth of its people adds to Shillong's charm, making it a destination that lingers in the hearts of its visitors.

Local Attractions

Umiam Lake

Umiam Lake, located 15 kilometers north of Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, holds a fascinating legend behind its name. In Khasi folklore, two sisters journeyed from heaven to Meghalaya, the 'abode of clouds,' but the younger sister lost her way. Overwhelmed with grief, the elder sister continuously shed tears, which formed the picturesque Umiam Lake. This man-made reservoir was created for hydroelectric power generation, surrounded by the lush green East Khasi hills, offering a stunning panorama for nature lovers. The lake's beauty is further accentuated by its surroundings, with thick coniferous forests and a vast expanse of 222 square kilometers. Visitors can enjoy the mesmerizing sunrise, explore the adjacent park for picnics, or partake in boating and water sports. The lake also provides a glimpse into the local way of life, with natives collecting twigs and grass, and fishermen gliding in their canoes across the tranquil waters. As seasons change, the shores of Umiam Lake transform; adorned with vibrant Gulmohar trees in summer and revealing an azure landscape as the water recedes in winter.

Elephant Falls

Named after an elephant-like stone at its base, Elephant Falls is a renowned waterfall near Shillong, popular for its three accessible layers. Originally called 'Ka Kshaid Lai Pateng Khohsiew' by the Khasi locals, meaning 'The Three Step Waterfalls,' it offers a picturesque sight with each tier having its unique charm. The first fall is broad, nestled amidst dense trees, while the second cascades down in thin strands, often less prominent in winter. The third and tallest fall is the most striking, with water flowing like a sheet of milk against dark rocks. Despite the disintegration of the elephant-shaped rock that gave the falls their name, the beauty of Elephant Falls remains captivating. It serves as an ideal spot to immerse oneself in nature's beauty and capture unforgettable moments. Just 12 km from Shillong, it is a popular stopover for tourists exploring Meghalaya's scenic wonders. The falls' allure, coupled with its easy accessibility, makes it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and photographers alike. The mesmerizing Elephant Falls, with its lush green surroundings and tranquil ambiance, is a testament to Meghalaya's natural beauty, offering visitors a glimpse of the state's rich biodiversity and stunning landscapes.

Shillong Peak

At the height of 6449 ft or 1965 m above sea level, Shillong Peak is the highest point of Shillong. It offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the entire city, the Himalayas, its waterfalls as well as of the Bangladesh plains. A telescope is available for tourists to get a bird's eye view. Trekking up to this semi-circular Shillong Peak is highly recommended for the best views but it is often bounded by heavy fog. It is said that Shillong gets its name from the Shillong Peak itself. Regional tales tell that the patron deity 'Leishyllong' resides in the hills and protects the city from all evils. The U Shulong site at the top of the peak is sacred and every spring, a ritual is organised there. The Shillong Peak is situated at the air force base and features a radar station of the Indian Air Force. Due to security reasons, heavy checking is done at the gates, and tourists are asked to deposit their cameras.

Location

Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, is located in northeastern India. Nestled in the East Khasi Hills, Shillong is known for its breathtaking landscapes and pleasant climate. The city is well-connected by road and air. The nearest airport is the Shillong Airport, also known as Umroi Airport, which is about 30 kilometers from the city center. The airport has limited connectivity, so many travelers prefer to fly to Guwahati, which is about 120 kilometers away, and then take a taxi or bus to Shillong. The city is also well-connected by road, with regular bus services from Guwahati and other nearby cities. Additionally, Shillong is a popular tourist destination and offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget, making it an ideal getaway for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Accommodation

Shillong offers a range of accommodation options to suit every traveler's needs and budget. From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses, the city has something for everyone. Hotel Polo Towers is a popular choice for luxury accommodation, offering modern amenities and stunning views of the city. For those looking for a more budget-friendly option, OYO Rooms and Airbnb have a variety of options available. Additionally, there are several guesthouses and homestays in and around the city that offer a more authentic experience of Shillong. Whether you're looking for a luxurious stay or a cozy homestay, Shillong has plenty of options to choose from.

Local Foods and Restaurants

Shillong fosters the flavours, colours, elements and popular dishes of an authentic Meghalayan cuisine. Do try the local preparations such as Tungtab which is a preparation of fermented dry fish, a wide variety of Momos, Tarumbai, which are fermented beans as well as the very popular Dohneiiong, a preparation of pork using local spices. You must also indulge in Kyat which is a beverage prepared from rice. A traditional and refreshing item is the Kwai prepared from betel nuts. Other than these Shillong's restaurants have enough choices from Chinese, Indian and Tibetan cuisines.

Shillong boasts a vibrant culinary scene with a variety of restaurants offering delectable dishes. Lamee Restaurant is renowned for its fusion cuisine, blending local flavors with global influences. Cafe Shillong is a cozy spot known for its excellent coffee and baked goods. Delhi Mistan Bhandar is a must-visit for those craving authentic Indian sweets and snacks. City Hut Dhaba offers a taste of North Indian cuisine, known for its hearty Punjabi dishes. These restaurants, among others, contribute to Shillong's diverse gastronomic landscape, making it a delightful destination for food enthusiasts.
