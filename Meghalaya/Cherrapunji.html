About Cherrapunji

Nestled amidst the misty hills of Meghalaya, Cherrapunji was once renowned as the wettest place on Earth. Located approximately 50 kilometers from Shillong, this sub-divisional town boasts a rich tapestry of flora and natural wonders. Although Mawsynram now holds the title of the wettest place, Cherrapunji retains its allure with the record for the highest recorded rainfall in a calendar year. Its iconic double-decker living root bridge, a marvel of indigenous engineering, stands as a testament to the region's unique heritage.

Local Attractions

Double Decker Living Root Bridge

Hidden deep in the lush, green forests of the wettest place in India: Cherrapunjee, Meghalaya; one can find the magnificent Jingkieng Nongriat Double Decker Living Root Bridge; more commonly known as the Umshiang Double Decker Living Root Bridge or simply just the Double Decker Root Bridge. This area is famous for root bridges made of Indian Rubber Tree, but the Double Decker Bridge is the most famous of all due to its large size. It is 3 kilometers long and placed at a height of 2400 feet. A unique attraction and quite an innovative mix of nature and engineering. The Umshiang River flows beneath the bridge.

Note : The hike down to the bridge is a whopping 3500-3600 steps and requires average fitness.

Arwah Cave

Nestled within the rugged terrain of Cherrapunji, Arwah Cave offers a captivating glimpse into Meghalaya's geological wonders. Renowned for its unique rock formations and fossil-rich interiors, the cave beckons adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts with an entrance reminiscent of Arabian Nights, leading to a labyrinth of caverns. Accessible to regular explorers, it showcases crustacean shells and fish bones embedded in limestone walls. Visitors navigate through twisting passageways and expansive chambers, encountering towering cave ceilings and narrow corridors shaped by millennia of flowing underwater streams. The picturesque trail to Arwah Cave winds through lush vegetation, offering scenic vistas of Lawshynna Hill and cascading waterfalls, with seating areas along the path inviting travelers to pause and soak in the natural beauty before exploring the cave's hidden treasures.

Location

Cherrapunji, a village in southern Meghalaya, northeastern India, is located on the Shillong Plateau about 35 miles (55 km) southwest of Shillong, the state capital. Officially known by its older name, Sohra, Cherrapunji is said to mean "land of oranges."

Tourists flock to Cherrapunji year-round, drawn by its perpetual monsoon and pleasant, comfortable weather. This quaint hill station in Meghalaya offers multiple reasons to be on your bucket list. While its year-long rains are a highlight, the best time to visit Cherrapunji is during the winter, between October and February. Early summer also provides an excellent opportunity to explore this northeastern gem of India.

Activities

Go for Caving

The thrill of exploring caves is something no other activity can evoke, and if you are into caving, Cherrapunji must be on your bucket list since it claims some of the largest caves in the country. The town of Cherrapunji has a wealth of natural caves that draw in scores of patrons of archaeology here to have a look at the natural marvels that date back to ancient times. If you are planning a caving expedition in Cherrapunji, you must know that you have some excellent options in and around the town, namely KremMawmluh Caves, Mawsmai Caves, KremLiatPrah Caves, and Arwah Caves. These caves feature striking rock formations and stalagmites.

Ziplining at Mawdok

Combine the thrill of adventure with the fun of relishing breathtaking vistas of a scenic valley by undertaking the activity of ziplining at Mandowk. Availed by locals and tourists alike, the activity is one of the most fun things to do in Cherrapunji. The activity is managed by a well-trained staff which will make sure you enjoy every bit of the experience and stay safe. The experience is available in various levels so that you can opt for the level of thrill your soul desires. If you prefer active travel, ziplining in Cherrapunji is an activity you must not skip.

Enjoy River Canyoning at Laitkynsew Hills

Among all the activities you can try in Cherrapunji, river canyoning is the most suitable one for daredevils in the town. The activity involves, crossing the river, trekking by the river, climbing boulders, and much more. However, the activity needs one to have a knack for the adventure and a willingness to embrace thrill since it can get demanding at some points and test your mettle. With all the heart-thumping and adrenaline-pumping moments, river canyoning in Cherrapunji will definitely make your visit to the town a memorable affair. Do not miss out on this fun-filled adventure activity that will also bring you face to face with raw nature.
