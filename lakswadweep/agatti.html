Agatti Island

A pristine coral island in Lakshadweep, known for its turquoise lagoons and abundant marine life

Introduction to Agatti Island

Agatti Island, located in the Lakshadweep archipelago, is celebrated for its stunning coral reefs, crystal-clear waters, and secluded beaches. It is a favored destination for nature enthusiasts and water sports aficionados alike.

Attractions and Activities

Agatti offers a variety of activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, and deep-sea fishing. Visitors can explore its rich underwater world teeming with colorful fish, turtles, and vibrant coral formations.

Local Culture and Cuisine

The culture of Agatti Island reflects the heritage of its local population, influenced by Kerala and the Malabar Coast. Travelers can savor traditional Maldivian cuisine and enjoy cultural performances amidst the island's serene environment.

Conservation Efforts

Agatti Island prioritizes sustainable tourism practices, focusing on preserving its fragile ecosystems and protecting endangered species like sea turtles and migratory birds. Conservation initiatives include waste management programs and responsible tourism guidelines.
