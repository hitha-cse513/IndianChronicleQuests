Overview

At a height of 1465 meters, Reiek Tlang or simply Reiek is one of the highest hills of Mizoram. Located at a distance of 29 km away from the city of Aizawl, Reiek Tlang is an excellent viewpoint overlooking panoramic views of Aizawl and the surrounding valley with the backdrop of sceneries borrowed from the Mizo Hills. The journey to the peak is also an excellent trekking opportunity usually reserved for calm sunny days. As is the case with most North Eastern destination of India, the peak of Reiek is also relatively unexplored and sits pretty as a pearl in the hills. The Reiek Hills are also home to a tiny village inhabited by Mizos which is considered to be a heritage site. Apart from sightseeing, the hill is also a haven for birdwatchers. While climbing up the hill one will come across nests and chirping of the native birds including the Peregrine Falcon which is known to make nests at the rocky edges.

History

Reiek, nestled in the heart of Mizoram, India, boasts a rich history intertwined with the vibrant Mizo culture. Historically, Reiek served as a strategic lookout point for Mizo warriors, offering a commanding view of the surrounding landscape. This vantage point allowed them to spot potential threats and protect their communities. Today, Reiek stands as a symbol of Mizo heritage, with its traditional bamboo dance and preserved way of life.

Over the years, Reiek has evolved into a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its natural beauty and cultural significance. The town's traditional bamboo huts and warm hospitality offer a glimpse into the Mizo way of life. The surrounding hills and forests provide a stunning backdrop for trekking and exploring, making Reiek a haven for nature enthusiasts. The town's history and culture are cherished and celebrated, making it a unique destination that offers a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

Places to Visit

Zopar Farm

Zopar Farm, on the way to Reiek in Mizoram, is known for its high-quality produce. Specializing in anthurium flowers, roses, and strawberries, the farm supplies these products locally and exports them to Kolkata. Anthurium flowers, valued for their exotic beauty, are popular for decorations. Roses add to the farm's floral variety, while strawberries offer a fresh treat for visitors. Zopar Farm's success highlights Mizoram's thriving horticulture industry and the skill of local farmers.

Ailawng Village

Ailawng is a charming village that has a lot of adventure activities to offer. One can opt for a cave exploration of Khuangchera Puk. It is the most technical cave to navigate in Mizoram and takes approximately 45 minutes to explore. The cave is 162 metres long and the height varies at 10 metres. It is named after Khuangchera, the legendary Mizo warrior known for his bravery and hunting feat who was the first explorer. On the outskirts lies a hanging bridge that offers a panoramic view of the green folded hills far up to the horizon against the pristine blue sky.

Reiek Peak

A hike up to Reiek Peak will take about 50 minutes. It is one of the most popular destinations for bird watching and is an ecotourism hotspot teeming with a variety of flora, fauna and wildlife. The hike up to the peak offers one a pristine view of the surrounding lush ancient forest preserved by the locals since the days of the chiefs. On a clear day, one can see Aizawl city on the horizon. Before descending the climb, witness the most spectacular orange flamed sunset against the backdrop of the rolling hills which is a feast for the eyes.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Reiek is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is cool and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities like trekking and sightseeing. The monsoon season, from June to September, should be avoided due to heavy rainfall, which can make the roads slippery and difficult to navigate.

Location

Reiek is located in the state of Mizoram, India, approximately 29 kilometers from Aizawl, the capital city. It is situated amidst the picturesque hills of Mizoram, offering stunning views and a serene environment for visitors. The town is easily accessible by road from Aizawl, making it a popular destination for tourists seeking a peaceful retreat amidst nature.

Contribution to Sustainable Development Goals

The Reiek Tourism Board has been empowered by the government to take a more proactive role in conserving natural and cultural resources. This includes organizing awareness programs and supporting the ban on single-use plastic. Workshops, seminars, and skill-building programs are being conducted in collaboration with the Government of Mizoram to bridge the knowledge gap and promote sustainable practices.

These efforts aim to provide local communities with additional sources of livelihood, empowering them to protect and conserve their cultural and natural heritage. By involving local communities as key stakeholders, there is a greater sense of responsibility and ownership in preserving these resources. This approach not only promotes environmentally friendly practices, such as refilling water bottles instead of using single-use plastic, but also encourages visitors to adopt these practices. Overall, local communities play a crucial role in promoting responsible and sustainable tourism through their active participation in cleanliness drives, awareness campaigns, and initiatives to reduce plastic waste.
