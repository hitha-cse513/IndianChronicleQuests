The Rice Bowl of Mizoram

Overview

Located on Mizoram's eastern border near the Indo-Myanmar border, Champhai is the administrative headquarters of the Champhai district, situated 188 kilometers from Aizawl. This town holds strategic and commercial importance for both Mizoram and India. Champhai and its surroundings are renowned for their natural beauty, characterized by vast valleys of rice fields, earning it the moniker 'Rice Bowl of Mizoram.' The area is also known for its vibrant seasonal orchids, adding to its aesthetic appeal and romantic ambiance. Champhai boasts several tourist attractions, primarily natural wonders. These include the Kungawrhi Puk cave, the picturesque Tiau Lui river, the scenic Lianchhiari Lunglen Tlang, and more. The famous Rih Dil Lake is another natural marvel near Champhai. Adventure enthusiasts can explore trekking opportunities at Thasiama Seno Neihna, located approximately 83 kilometers south of Champhai.

History

Champhai is one of the oldest places in Mizoram which the native people have occupied. The history of Champhai traces back to the British period. The first Mizo People who came from the east during 1500-1800 AD to Mizoram were Fenngo clan. The town also served as the headquarters of Lalbura Sailo, a Mizo Chief against whom the Expedition of the British during of 1871-72 was directed. During the British period, Champhai also received the status of a fort.

Places to Visit

Rih Dil Lake

Rih Dil, situated near Champhai in Mizoram, is a captivating lake known for its natural beauty and cultural significance. This heart-shaped lake is surrounded by picturesque hills and verdant forests, creating a serene and tranquil atmosphere that attracts tourists seeking a peaceful retreat. Apart from its scenic charm, Rih Dil is also steeped in local folklore and legends, adding to its allure. According to Mizo folklore, the lake is believed to be the passage for departed souls to the afterlife. This mythological significance adds a layer of mystique to the lake, making it a fascinating destination for visitors interested in local culture and traditions. Visitors to Rih Dil can enjoy the scenic beauty of the lake, surrounded by lush greenery and offering a peaceful environment for relaxation and contemplation. The area around the lake is also ideal for nature walks and trekking, allowing visitors to explore the natural beauty of the region. Overall, Rih Dil is not only a beautiful natural attraction but also a place of cultural and historical importance, making it a must-visit destination for tourists exploring the enchanting land of Mizoram.

Lianchhiari Lunglen Tlang

Lianchhiari Lunglen Tlang, located in Champhai, Mizoram, is a captivating hill known for its scenic beauty and lush greenery. The name "Lianchhiari" translates to "a place to rest," and true to its name, the hill offers a serene and tranquil environment, ideal for relaxation and rejuvenation. The hill is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. Visitors can enjoy leisurely walks along the hill's paths, soaking in the natural beauty and peaceful ambiance. Lianchhiari Lunglen Tlang is also known for its vibrant flora, with a variety of colorful flowers blooming throughout the year. This adds to the hill's charm, making it a picturesque spot for nature lovers and photographers. Overall, Lianchhiari Lunglen Tlang is a hidden gem in Champhai, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty and tranquility, making it a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the enchanting landscapes of Mizoram.

Thasiama Seno Neihna

Thasiama Seno Neihna, located approximately 83 kilometers south of Champhai in Mizoram, is a mesmerizing plateau renowned for its natural beauty and serene atmosphere. The name "Thasiama Seno Neihna" translates to "Great Stone of Devotion" in the local Mizo language, reflecting the spiritual significance of the place. This plateau is a popular destination for trekking enthusiasts, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The trek to Thasiama Seno Neihna is an adventure in itself, with the journey taking visitors through lush forests and picturesque landscapes. One of the highlights of Thasiama Seno Neihna is its unique rock formations, which are believed to have been shaped by natural forces over thousands of years. These rock formations add to the mystique of the place, making it a fascinating destination for nature lovers and photographers. The plateau is also home to a variety of flora and fauna, adding to its natural beauty and ecological importance. Visitors to Thasiama Seno Neihna can experience the tranquility of nature, away from the hustle and bustle of city life, making it an ideal destination for those seeking peace and solitude.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Champhai is during the months of October to April when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. During these months, the temperature is moderate, and the skies are usually clear, offering excellent views of the surrounding landscapes. On the other hand, the monsoon season, from May to September, is not preferable for visiting Champhai. During this time, the region experiences heavy rainfall, which can lead to landslides and road blockages, making travel difficult and unsafe. Additionally, the incessant rain may limit outdoor activities and sightseeing opportunities.

The town's vibrant seasonal orchids add to its aesthetic appeal, making it a picturesque destination in Mizoram.
